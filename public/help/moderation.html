Moderation & Community Standards
A Quick Guide for Users

Welcome to our platform.
We’re here to support bold, creative expression — and a healthy, productive public space.
This guide explains how moderation works, how to flag content, and how to appeal decisions, so that everyone knows exactly what to expect.

1. What Our Moderation System Is (and Isn’t)

Moderation on our platform is designed to be:

  • Transparent
  • Fair
  • Documented
  • Appealable

Every moderation action must cite a specific rule from our published Community Guidelines.
You will always know what rule applied and why.

We do not use secret rules or hidden enforcement.

2. What You Can Report

You may report content that appears to violate the Community Guidelines, including:

  • Harassment or targeted abuse
  • Hateful expressions based on identity
  • Dangerous misinformation
  • Explicit content in non-mature channels
  • Illegal content
  • Threats or incitement
  • Spam or coordinated disruption

When in doubt, check the Channel Rules — some channels allow “spicy,” satirical, or mature content, and others do not.

3. How Reporting Works

  1. Your report is logged (along with your credibility score).
  2. The creator is notified and offered two options:
    • Concede the violation
    • Appeal the report
  3. If they concede: The item is removed and the reporter receives a small reward.
  4. If they appeal: A trained reviewer evaluates the item and issues a ruling based on the guidelines.

This process discourages abuse, reduces false reports, and creates accountability on all sides.

4. Credibility Scores

Every user has a Credibility Score that affects visibility and reach.

You gain credibility by:

  • Posting guideline-compliant content
  • Correctly flagging violations
  • Participating constructively
  • Accepting AI prompts to rephrase inflammatory comments
  • Conceding when you make a mistake

You may lose credibility if:

  • You repeatedly make false reports
  • You engage in coordinated mass-flagging
  • Your content repeatedly violates guidelines
  • You ignore AI warnings for severe language issues

Higher credibility unlocks:

  • Wider reach
  • Comment priority
  • Channel recognition badges
  • Access to advanced community tools

5. The Appeals Process

If you receive a moderation action, you may:

A. Accept the decision

Your content is removed, and the case is closed.

B. Appeal the moderation ruling

A reviewer will examine:

  • The content
  • The context
  • The relevant guidelines
  • Current channel rules

Their decision will cite the exact rule applied.
You will always receive a written explanation.

Repeated misuse of appeals (including frivolous appeals) may lower your credibility score.

6. AI Rephrase Assistant

Before submitting a comment, you may see optional suggestions such as:

  • “This sentence may be interpreted as harassment.”
  • “Would you like to add context to avoid misinterpretation?”
  • “Try softening this line to keep the conversation productive.”

These are tools, not punishments.
Using them improves your credibility and helps keep conversations meaningful.

7. Why We Do Moderation This Way

Our goals are to:

  • Support robust, diverse, open expression
  • Protect users from harassment and misinformation
  • Provide advertisers clarity and predictable environments
  • Offer creators clear, consistent expectations
  • Maintain public trust
  • Model the kind of discourse we want to encourage

We don’t believe in opaque bans or mysterious enforcement.
We believe in process, transparency, and dialogue.
